Mitsuha Miyamizu

the main female protagonist of Your Name. and makes a cameo appearance in Weathering with You. She is a 17-year-old girl living in the rural town of Itomori who yearns for a life in the bustling city of Tokyo.

Taki Tachibana

the male protagonist of Kimi no Na wa.. He is a 17-year old high-school student who lives a busy lifestyle as he juggles his part-time job at an Italian restaurant, spends fun times with his friends and hopes for a future in architecture.

Miki Okudera


She is one of Taki Tachibana's colleagues who work at the Italian restaurant, and they have a mutual crush on each other for the first part of the movie.

Tsukasa Fujii

He is one of Taki Tachibana's closests friends who is often concerned about him whenever Mitsuha Miyamizu embodies him. At the end of the movie, he is married to Miki Okudera.

Yotsuha Miyamizu

She is Mitsuha's younger sister, an elementary-school student, and a member of the Miyamizu family, where she participates in rituals for her family's shrine, including, but not limited to, creating kuchikamizake as a shrine maiden.

Katsuhiko Teshigawara


He is a close friend of Mitsuha Miyamizu and Sayaka Natori, as well as an expert with machinery; particularly explosive

Sayaka Natori

She is a gentle and quiet girl who is also a member of the broadcast club in high school. She is a side character in the Kimi no Na wa. film. Sayaka is a teenage girl with average build and height.

Hitoha Miyamizu

is the head of the Miyamizu family and the maternal grandmother of Mitsuha Miyamizu and Yotsuha Miyamizu who began raising them herself after the death of Futaba Miyamizu and Toshiki Miyamizu's decision to pursue a career in politics.

Toshiki Miyamizu

is shown to be a very busy man and the estranged father of Mitsuha and Yotsuha Miyamizu. Following his wife's, Futaba's, death, he rejected the Miyamizu shrine and its traditions to pursue a career in politics. He became the mayor of Itomori two years after his wife's death and has held the position for four years as of 2013.
